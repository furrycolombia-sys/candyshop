### [SEC-004] Content Security Policy uses 'unsafe-inline' and 'unsafe-eval' in script-src across all apps

- **File:** `apps/studio/next.config.ts`
- **Line:** 46
- **Description:** All 7 apps (studio, landing, payments, playground, auth, admin, store) configure their CSP with `script-src 'self' 'unsafe-inline' 'unsafe-eval'`. This effectively disables the XSS protection benefit of CSP for scripts. `'unsafe-eval'` allows eval()-based code execution, and `'unsafe-inline'` allows inline event handlers and script tags. Combined, these directives mean any XSS vulnerability would not be mitigated by CSP. The same pattern is repeated in all apps: `apps/auth/next.config.ts`, `apps/admin/next.config.ts`, `apps/store/next.config.ts`, `apps/payments/next.config.ts`, `apps/landing/next.config.ts`, `apps/playground/next.config.ts`.
- **Fix:** Replace `'unsafe-inline'` and `'unsafe-eval'` with a nonce-based or hash-based CSP. Next.js supports nonce-based CSP via middleware. `'unsafe-eval'` may be required by some third-party libraries but should be removed where possible. At minimum, document why these directives are necessary.
