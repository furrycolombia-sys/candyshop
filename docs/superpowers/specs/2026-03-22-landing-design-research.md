# Landing Page Design Research

**Date:** 2026-03-22
**Purpose:** Research references for a furry community marketplace landing page with camp/neobrutalist aesthetic.

---

## Chosen Direction: Neobrutalism + Corporate Colors

The landing page uses a neobrutalist frame (thick borders, hard offset shadows, square corners) with the corporate green/gold palette from the mascot logo. Camp energy comes from the bold graphic treatment, not from emoji or decorative clutter.

## Neobrutalist Design Tokens

### Borders

- Standard: `3px solid #000`
- Thin: `2px solid #000`
- Thick: `4px solid #000`

### Shadows (hard offset, zero blur)

- Small: `3px 3px 0 0 #000`
- Medium: `5px 5px 0 0 #000`
- Large: `8px 8px 0 0 #000`
- XL: `12px 12px 0 0 #000`

### Border Radius

- `0` (square corners only)

### Hover States

- Translate: `translate(-2px, -2px)` + shadow growth
- Active/press: `translate(3px, 3px)` + shadow removal

### Typography

- Display: **Syne 800** (oversized, chunky, personality)
- Body: readable sans-serif (DM Sans, etc.)

## Furry Community Site References

### Itaku.ee

- Dark charcoal bg (#303030), white text, Material dark theme
- Community-first, no marketing fluff
- Dark mode signals "for creators, not shoppers"

### Toyhou.se

- Multiple user-selectable themes (Night, Bee, Velvet)
- Deep HTML/CSS customization for profiles
- Community creates design categories: Kidcore, Cottagecore, Vaporwave, Scene
- Platform stays humble so user content shines

### Art Fight

- Yearly team themes (Sun vs Moon, Coffee vs Tea, Steam vs Cyberpunk)
- Each year new two-tone palette for competing teams
- Gamification of art gifting, seasonal anticipation

### itch.io

- Sparse decoration, orange accent for sales
- User-generated tag system
- Customizable store pages — "nothing on it you didn't put there"
- Content IS the design

### Ko-fi

- Warm orange/coral accent on white
- Non-judgmental, inclusive creator celebration

### Big Cartel

- Custom fonts (es-replan-variform, abc-marist) for personality
- "Real humans, not chatbots" voice

## Camp Aesthetic Rules

1. **Self-awareness is key** — camp knows it's excessive and that IS the point
2. **Commit to the bit** — half-measures read as corporate trying to be fun
3. **Saturated, clashing colors** — visual discord is intentional
4. **Bold shapes, thick lines** — sticker/zine energy
5. **Bouncy interactions** — hard shadow hover states
6. **Anti-corporate** — no glassmorphism, gradient blurs, stock photography

### What NOT to do

- No emoji as icons (feels like kids' menu)
- No floating blob backgrounds (corporate SaaS)
- No "trusted by 10,000+ creators" social proof
- No minimalist grey-on-white (AI slop)
- No trying to be fun — just BE fun through craft
